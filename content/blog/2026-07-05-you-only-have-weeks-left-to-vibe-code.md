Waiting for perfection is just another way of hiding. You tell yourself you are tweaking the layout or fixing a minor alignment, but you are really just making excuses. You are wasting time.

A design sitting on your computer is useless. It does not matter how good it looks in your design software. It only matters how it works in the real world. Real users will tell you what is broken in five minutes. You could stare at your screen for five days and miss it entirely.

![](/assets/uploads/526743668_767130302487992_7141786749365973708_n.jpg)

How to ship faster:

* Focus on the core function. Does the page load? Do the links work? Can people read the text? If yes, it is ready.
* Stop adding things. Every time you want to add a new animation or a cool hover effect, ask yourself if it actually helps the user. Usually, it does not.
* Launch first, fix later. Put the work out there. Find the real problems and fix them tomorrow.

Your goal is to build a working machine. Get the engine running, put it in front of people, and let the real world show you what to do next. Stop stalling and hit deploy.

Stop waiting for a massive, game-changing project to drop into your lap. A lot of creatives have empty portfolios because they think every piece of work has to be a masterpiece. That is a quick way to stay invisible.

The people who actually get hired and make money are not always the most talented. They are the most consistent. They show up every day, they build small things, and they put those things out into the world.

When you only focus on big wins, you get paralyzed. You stop experimenting because the stakes feel too high.

How to fix this right now:

* Work small. Design a single button. Write one short blog post. Fix one broken layout. Do not try to build a whole system in one day.
* Post your messy work. Let people see your process. Clients want to see how you think and how you solve problems, not just the shiny final product.
* Do it every single day. A small update every day adds up to a massive body of work by the end of the year.

Stop overthinking your grand entrance. Just put your head down, do the work, and hit publish.

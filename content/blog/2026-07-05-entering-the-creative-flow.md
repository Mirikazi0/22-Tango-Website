A lot of people think design is just picking the right colors or making a cool logo. But if we are being honest, that is just decoration. Real design is about solving a problem.

Think about it like building a house. You wouldn't pick out the curtains before you pour the foundation. The same goes for building a website, an app, or a brand. You have to figure out how it works before you decide how it looks.

When you just focus on making things pretty, you end up with a broken machine hidden under a nice coat of paint. It might get a few likes online, but it won't actually help anyone do their job or buy your product.

Here is what you should focus on first:

* Who is using this? Figure out what they actually need, not just what you want to build.
* How does it work? Map out the steps. Keep it simple and straightforward.
* Is it fast? Nobody waits for a slow page to load, no matter how good it looks.

Once you have the bones in place and the machine is working perfectly, then you can start painting the walls. Keep your tools simple, keep your process clean, and always focus on how the end user experiences the product.
